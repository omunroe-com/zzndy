Royalty Tax Terms

ASSUMED TERMS AT A GLANCE

FISCAL REGIME

  

Norway - Royalty Tax Terms

BONUSES

  

No bonuses

OTHER PAYMENTS

  

After an initial period of 10 years an annual rental is payable on a sliding scale from NOK 7,000/km2 to a maximum of NOK 70,000/km2

STATE PARTICIPATION

  

State participates from day one as a working interest partner.  The percentage participation is variable and determined licence-by-licence.  20% is typical

ROYALTY

  

None
COST RECOVERY   The fiscal regime does not provide for production sharing
PROFIT SHARING   The fiscal regime does not provide for production sharing

INCOME TAX

  

Known as corporate income tax and levied on gross revenue less deductions and depreciation.  The income tax rate is 28% and is set annually by legislation

ADDITIONAL PROFITS
 TAX

  

Known as special petroleum tax and levied on gross revenue less deductions and depreciation.  The APT rate is 50% and is set annually by legislation

DOMESTIC SUPPLY
OBLIGATION

  

None

WITHHOLDING TAX

  

None

 

BONUS & OTHER PAYMENTS

There is no provision for the payment of signature, discovery, or production bonuses.

There is no provision for the payment of training fees.

Note:  After initial period of 10 years an annual rental is payable on a sliding scale from NOK 7,000/km2 to a maximum NOK 70,000/km2 but these have not been modelled here.

STATE PARTICIPATION

State takes a direct financial interest (DFI) from day one.  In addition the state oil company may participate in licences but it does so on equal basis with private investors and therefore has not been modelled.  State participation of 20% has been assumed, reflecting some of the preliminary awards of the 18th Licensing Round.

ROYALTY

None.

Note:  Royalty has been levied on gross production at rates between 8% and 16% but it it has been abolished as far as most fields are concerned but it still has to be paid on oil production from two fields, namely Oseberg and Gullfaks.  For these fields royalty will be removed by 2005.

CARBON DIOXIDE TAX

Carbon dioxide tax is levied on the volume of petroleum flared, on the volume of natural gas vented, and on CO2 separated from petroleum and vented on platforms or other installations used for production or transportation of petroleum.  The rate of carbon dioxide tax in recent years is as follows:

Tax (Financial) Year  Carbon Dioxide Tax NOK/ m3 Carbon Dioxide Tax $ / mcf *
1999 0.89 3.36
2000 0.70 2.64
2001 0.72 2.72
2004 0.76 3.12
2005 0.78 3.40

* Assuming the exchange rate prevailing in January of the Tax Year and 1 m3 = 35.3 cubic feet.

Carbon dioxide tax is deductible for income tax and special petroleum tax.

Note:  The reduction announced in 1999 (from NOK 0.89 to NOK 0.70/m3) was the first step in an ongoing activity to align the CO2 issue with EU and Global Emission standards. Eventually, the CO2 Tax as such will be abolished and replaced by tradable "Emission Quotas" which companies will either use themselves or sell/trade to others who may have over-utilised their own quota.

INCOME TAX

Known as corporate income tax and levied on gross revenue (see Note 1) less exploration costs (see Note 2), operating costs, royalty, carbon dioxide tax, and depreciation of development costs.  The annual depreciation rate is 16 2/3% (i.e. 6 years straight-line) starting from the year the investment was made.  Although the legislation provides for losses to be carried forward for up to 15 years, in practise losses may be carried forward indefinitely.  Losses from 2002 onwards may be carried forward with interest (calculated as a risk-free interest plus a margin after deducting income tax at the prevailing income tax rate).  The income tax rate is 28%.

Notes 1:  There is extensive integration between buyers and sellers in the petroleum sector.  Consequently, for tax purposes, a system of norm prices (i.e. administratively fixed petroleum prices) has been introduced. The norm price is supposed to reflect the price that petroleum could have been traded at between independent parties in a free market.  At present norm prices are stipulated only for crude oil.  Gas sales are taxed on the basis of the actual realized sales price.

Note 2:  Exploration costs may be expensed and deducted immediately or capitalised and depreciated.

ADDITIONAL PROFITS TAX

Known as special petroleum tax, the taxable base for additional profits tax is the same as for income tax but includes an extra allowance in the form of an uplift.  The uplift was originally  equal to 5% of the capital investment (i.e. development costs and capitalised interest but not exploration costs) for 6 years starting from the year in which the investment was made (i.e. 130% of development costs and capitalised interest are depreciated over 6 years straight-line).  If the uplift exceeds the income subject to special tax, excess uplift may be deducted in subsequent years.  The treatment of losses for SPT is the same as for income tax.  The SPT rate is 50%.

Note:  The purpose of the uplift is to ensure normal returns to the investor are not adversely affected by SPT.  Beginning 1 January 2005, under Act No. 78 of 10 December 2004 the uplift was accelerated to 7.5% per annum over a 4 year period from the year the investment was made but this acceleration has not been modelled.

WITHHOLDING TAX

None payable.

DECOMMISSIONING COSTS

All costs relating to decommissioning activities on the Norwegian continental shelf are deductible for tax.  If carried forward losses include decommissioning costs, these are to be recorded separately.  When taxable activities cease, the tax value from any remaining carried-forward losses from decommissioning will be reimbursed by the state.

RING FENCE

Around the field for royalty; around upstream petroleum activities for income tax and additional profits tax.

Note:  The ring fence of the Petroleum Tax Act is around sub-sea exploration, production, and transportation activities in offshore waters as well as related onshore pipeline transportation and other activities at reception and shipment facilities (i.e. around upstream petroleum activities).

Quality

Fixed terms except state participation, which is variable - indicative rate from 18th Licensing Round (2004) assumed.
